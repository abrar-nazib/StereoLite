\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

\IEEEPARstart{D}{eep} stereo matching has progressed through three paradigms in under a decade. End-to-end neural networks trained on synthetic data replaced the classical pipeline of Scharstein and Szeliski~\cite{scharstein2002taxonomy}: MC-CNN~\cite{zbontar2016mccnn} learned matching costs, DispNetC~\cite{mayer2016sceneflow} learned full disparity regression from a 2D correlation volume, and a 3D-cost-volume lineage (GC-Net~\cite{kendall2017gcnet}, PSMNet~\cite{chang2018psmnet}, GA-Net~\cite{zhang2019ganet}, GwcNet~\cite{guo2019gwcnet}) established the regularisation-by-3D-convolution recipe that dominated benchmarks for several years. RAFT-Stereo~\cite{lipson2021raftstereo} then replaced single-pass regression with iterative refinement; CREStereo~\cite{li2022crestereo}, IGEV-Stereo~\cite{xu2023igev}, Selective-Stereo~\cite{wang2024selective} and IGEV++~\cite{xu2025igevpp} extended the iterative paradigm and brought the KITTI 2015 D1-all error~\cite{menze2015kitti} below 1.6\%. Most recently, a foundation-model era has emerged in which a monocular depth foundation~\cite{yang2024dav2} is fused with a stereo matcher; DEFOM-Stereo~\cite{jiang2025defom}, FoundationStereo~\cite{wen2025foundationstereo}, MonSter~\cite{cheng2025monster}, and Stereo Anywhere~\cite{bartolomei2025stereoanywhere} share this design and deliver substantial zero-shot accuracy gains on Middlebury~\cite{scharstein2014middlebury}, ETH3D~\cite{schops2017eth3d}, and Booster~\cite{ramirez2022booster}. Fig.~\ref{fig:timeline} summarises the paradigm timeline.

The accuracy of foundation-based stereo comes at a substantial cost. DEFOM-Stereo~\cite{jiang2025defom}, FoundationStereo~\cite{wen2025foundationstereo}, and MonSter~\cite{cheng2025monster} all use a Depth Anything V2~\cite{yang2024dav2} ViT-Large backbone, which by itself contains over 300~M parameters and dominates inference. Reported inference times lie in the several-hundred-millisecond regime on a desktop GPU; the per-method numbers collated from the original publications appear in Table~\ref{tab:efficient_comparison} and Fig.~\ref{fig:pareto_kitti}. On contemporary embedded accelerators, Pip-Stereo~\cite{zheng2026pipstereo} reports that the full DEFOM-Stereo architecture cannot fit in the 8~GB unified memory of a Jetson Orin Nano at full precision. The gap between foundation-quality accuracy and embedded-accelerator latency is the natural subject of a focused review.

Several stereo surveys already exist: Scharstein and Szeliski~\cite{scharstein2002taxonomy} on the classical pipeline, Hirschm\"{u}ller~\cite{hirschmuller2008sgm} on SGM, Laga \emph{et al.}~\cite{laga2020survey} on the pre-iterative deep era, Poggi \emph{et al.}~\cite{poggi2021synergies} on the synergies between machine learning and binocular stereo, and Tosi \emph{et al.}~\cite{tosi2025survey} on the modern 2020s era. None treats edge deployment as the primary lens, and Tosi \emph{et al.}'s was finalised before the 2025--2026 wave of compression-oriented work (Pip-Stereo~\cite{zheng2026pipstereo}, Distill-then-Prune~\cite{pan2024dtp}, Fast-FoundationStereo~\cite{wen2026fastfoundationstereo}, BANet~\cite{xu2025banet}, GGEV~\cite{liu2026ggev}, LightStereo~\cite{guo2025lightstereo}, LiteAnyStereo~\cite{jing2025liteanystereo}).

We use the term \emph{compression} broadly throughout, to mean any technique that reduces inference compute, parameter count, or memory footprint of a stereo network while attempting to preserve accuracy. We group the surveyed methods into seven compression families: backbone substitution, cost-volume compression, iterative-loop compression, knowledge distillation, architectural compression, adaptive compute, and neural architecture search. These families are not mutually exclusive (Pip-Stereo, for example, performs both iterative-loop compression and knowledge distillation), but each corresponds to a distinct cost axis being attacked, and we discuss each in isolation in Section~\ref{sec:taxonomy}. We exclude active stereo, event-camera stereo, and multi-view stereo from the scope, and do not treat generic model-compression techniques (pruning, quantisation, low-rank factorisation) except as they have been specialised to stereo.

The remainder of the paper is organised as follows. Section~\ref{sec:background} reviews the deep-stereo pipeline and the standard evaluation protocol. Section~\ref{sec:foundation} characterises the foundation-model accuracy envelope against which the compression literature is implicitly measured. Section~\ref{sec:taxonomy} is the technical core: it defines the seven compression families and surveys representative methods in each. Section~\ref{sec:generalization} treats cross-domain generalisation techniques that are relevant when a compressed model must be deployed outside its training distribution. Section~\ref{sec:edge_hardware} reviews the limited body of validation on actual embedded accelerators. Section~\ref{sec:roadmap} discusses open problems that the surveyed literature has not addressed. Section~\ref{sec:conclusion} concludes.
